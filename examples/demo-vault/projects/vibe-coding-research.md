# Vibe coding landscape — competitive research

_Status:_ Vibe-coding landscape March 2026. Our mobile pipeline is ahead in integration depth, at parity in orchestration, behind in IDE UX. Multi-model orchestration is commoditized.

> Competitive position of the mobile app pipeline vs the broader vibe-coding market.

## Details

**Position:** Ahead in integration depth. At parity in orchestration. Behind in IDE UX.

**Unique features (no competitor has all):**

- Full pipeline: market research → Pinterest refs → AI mockups → SwiftUI → ASO → App Store
- AI Polish Loop (model generates → vision critiques screenshot → iterate)
- Reference-driven design (Pinterest → image-gen → SwiftUI)
- Category-aware design recipes (Cookbook.md)

**Moat:** iOS domain knowledge in every phase, NOT orchestration pattern (commoditized).

**Platform crackdown context:** Replit + Vibecode blocked on App Store. Web-wrapped apps violate store rules. SwiftUI-first = safe.

**Closest competitor:** App Factory (10-stage pipeline, but React Native not SwiftUI).

**Multi-model orchestration:** 5+ open-source implementations. Pattern no longer novel. All generic, none iOS-specific.

**Why:** informs roadmap — focus on iOS depth, not orchestration features.
**How to apply:** don't compete on orchestration. Compete on iOS domain knowledge + SwiftUI quality + full pipeline.

## Gaps to consider

- SweetPad / XcodeBuildMCP for IDE integration
- Consensus gates (formal multi-model agreement)
- On-device Apple Foundation Models (Xcode 26.3)
